My Answers
  • Q1. System -A system is a group of entities working together in a common goal. Not a system would be two entities causing each other problems to reach a common goal.
  • Q2. Modularity - is a bunch of separate pieces working together as a whole. I think safe with gears and locks work in modularity because you can replace gears if it breaks. Decomposability- all systems can be broken down to a certain extent. (Relevancy) A map of the light rail station destinations breaks down to relevancy because it doesn’t show the exact distance nor the times. It only shows where it goes. Emergence- Is something coming to view or becoming exposed after being concealed. I think of a full garbage can on someone’s computer. Chaos theory- Is the theory that small conditions can create a series of large affected outcomes. Tapping the side of your soda cup will cause bubbles to come up and leave a different pattern.
  • Q3. I think the difference between modularity and tessellation is the purpose or function of it. Tessellations are designs that can be put together one way, where a modularity is based on function and a little more flexible.
  • Q4. Complexity has chaos and a bunch of different things going on and Uniform means they all do the same thing.
  • Q5. The only difference I see between a system diagram and an exploded view drawing is their purpose. A computer is a very complex system so a system diagram would show all of its functions and an exploded view of how to assemble a gear pump or the anatomy of a ladybug only has one function.
