Steven Canoy Art103: Reading 2 - Site Specific Work

“Roads? Where we're going we don't need roads.” -Back to the Future, 1985
Questions

Q1. What are the differences between Site Specific art work and the idea of Art Interventions.

  • I think site specific art relies more on its placement and how the environment around it enhances the art. Art interventions are artworks that can be site specific but they are repurposing the area itself.

Q2. Choose 2 of the 4 artists shared and answer the following for each artist.

  • I think site specific art relies more on its placement and how the environment around it enhances the art. Art interventions are artworks that can be site specific but they are repurposing the area itself.

A. Take one or more of the art works from the listed artists and please describe how a piece(s) function as site specific work or an intervention. Please provide context and examples to explain your answer.

  • The lightning rods from Walter de Maria are site specific because the specific area they are placed is known for having a lot of lightning strikes.

B. For the same piece(s), explain your interpretation of the work. What does it mean to you?

  • -I feel like the artwork is perfect for its location because it is too big to fit in a museum and it needs the lightning from the site. My first interpretation of the work is an energy generating field. It would be cool if they collected electricity from the lightning strikes to power a city grid.

Q3. What is the single most important that all signs must share to be relevant and effective? This means ALL signs, from art signs to traffic signs, to signs even in our home. This is NOT about aesthetics or construction, but just functionality of the idea.

  • The single most important thing that all signs must share is site specific. Where the sign is placed is specific to its function. If we take a bathroom sign and hang it up in our room then its purpose is no longer site specific.

Q4. What Kind of art, (not shown here), have you seen that was made in response to its location, its environment, a specific event or situation. Please elaborate on its description and provide 1 image for reference.

  • I have been to Rome, Italy and visited the Sistine Chapel. I believe the artwork painted by Michelangelo it is site specific because it is located in the Vatican church. If they were located in my house, it would lose its purpose because I don’t follow their beliefs.
Phase 1.2: A group exercise with Tesslation and Modularity in Illustrator

In this phase, we created tessellations on Adobe Illustrator and came up with our own design along with a common side that we all agreed on. The First image shows our individual designs and the second image shows variations of how they can tessellate.

Phase 2 3D Fastener BrainStorming Designs and Goldilocks Laser Tests

In phase 2 we tested different designs and materials, In my first attempt I used plike board and I tried to 'hand cut' throughole finger tenons. The design was really rough because I was not able to get little detailed cuts like a laser cutter, but the exercise helped me understand how it works. The second design was in adobe illustrator and I used the same method as the tessalation exercise to match the sizes and input the measurments to match the thickness of the board in order to give it a flush look.



Phase 3 Goldilocks Fastener tests: Intitial Designs and Cuts

The first cut was with finger slots and locked in a little tight because I nudged the inside corners in farther than I needed to. The second goldilocks test was with throughole finger tenons of my own design and I tried to have a center lock with one support on each side. The locks fit but were also a little too tight due to over nudging.



Final Project

For our final design, we created a 'build your own path' type of activity reguarding forest fires and called it REDWOOD, FIREWOOD. We came up with designs and shared them on discord. Zak and I worked on editing objects that were cutting off sections of the design because some of the lines were connected or too close to the edge that the objects would fall apart.



Artist Statement

For this project we had many ups and downs. Getting a chance to laser-cut became our biggest enemy. For example, we planned to laser cut on a Friday and after waiting for other groups to finish, it was finally our turn and just as we begin cutting, the wood-shop worker announced that they are closing in 10 minutes. So we didn’t get to cut much and there was no other place open on the weekend. Monday came and oak did some test cuts but we needed more wood so I took the day off to get supplies and get our pieces cut. The only problem was that the pieces were scaled down after we already added the fastener; therefore, the fastener was too small to fit on the board. I began editing everyones files with new fasteners and allowed others to laser-cut while I worked. Everyone made their designs differently so some took more editing than others because corners weren’t connected or lines were too close to the edge. Zak came after his morning class and began editing with me. Thankfully he was able to scale up the images and fit the fasteners. It was a rush against time again waiting for groups to finish cutting, but we managed to cut our pieces before getting in trouble for taking too long. Everyone helped with post production, Jessica made the schema and Diana make the slideshow, while Zak took photos and sent them to Jessica for our Video Editing. Some things that work are people helping and stepping up when needed but the different styles and ideas are hard to work with. You never know how group projects will go, there can be a person making all of the decisions or you can let other ideas join and find ways to incorperate them. Luckily my team was very adaptable and everyone had their special contribution.
